Cal‑Cook – Product Requirements Document

Product Overview

Cal‑Cook is a calendar‑synced meal‑planning and cooking‑execution app that reads the user’s day, understands available time windows, and converts that into a realistic meal plan plus a timeline‑based cooking to‑do list. The core promise: “Tell me what my day looks like, what I have at home, and what I can spend — then give me a feasible cooking plan.”

Goals & Success Metrics

  • Plan acceptance rate ≥ 80 % after first morning sync.
  • Budget adherence rate ≥ 85 %.
  • Average time to generate a daily plan ≤ 3 seconds.
  • User‑retention after 30 days ≥ 70 %.

Personas

  • Busy Professional – needs quick meals that fit a tight calendar.
  • Family Organizer – coordinates meals for multiple household members.
  • Health‑conscious Planner – tracks nutrition and budget.

End‑to‑End User Journey

  1. Onboarding – account creation, calendar connection, profile & budget setup.
  2. Morning sync – app reads today’s calendar, builds a feasibility model.
  3. Plan generation – recipes, cost, time, and substitution suggestions.
  4. Grocery list creation – pantry‑aware aggregation.
  5. Cooking timeline – auto‑scheduled tasks aligned with free windows.
  6. Execution – notifications, timers, and on‑the‑fly pivots.
  7. Learning – feedback loop improves future recommendations.

User Stories & Acceptance Criteria (sample)

As a Busy Professional, I want the app to generate a dinner plan that fits my 6 pm‑7 pm free slot.

  • Given a calendar event from 6 pm‑7 pm marked as “Free”, when I open the app after morning sync, the system should propose a dinner recipe whose total prep + cook time ≤ 60 minutes.
  • Acceptance: The suggested recipe’s estimated time is shown, and the cooking task list schedules the first step at ≤ 6:00 pm.

As a Family Organizer, I want to share the grocery list with my partner.

  • When I invite a household member via email, they receive a link granting “edit” permission on the grocery list.
  • Acceptance: Both users see real‑time updates; changes made by either user are reflected within 2 seconds.
